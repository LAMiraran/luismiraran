History of the Philippines


Pre-Colonial Philippines

The Philippines is believed to have Austronesian roots according to the similarities of migration and language patterns of neighboring countries such as Indonesia and Malaysia. Early settlements were divided and formed small communities known as “Barangays”; These barangays were led by a Datu. These barangays were linked to each other by kinship or in relation with other communities that formed allegiances. Since communities are divided, Filipinos haven’t grasped the sense of Nationalism.

Spanish Rule

Ferdinand Magellan’s arrival to the Philippines in 1521 was later continued by Miguel Lopez de Legazpi in 1565 paving the way for power and conversion of religion. The Spanish colony contributed to the progress of the Religion, Education, and Culture of Filipinos. However, the colonizers abused their power such as the ruling of the Encomienda System which led to excessive taxation, slavery, and exploitation of resources and land of the locals. Occurrences of inequality in education, opportunities, and even religion hindered Filipinos. Despite that, in the 1870s the emergence of Ilustrados, or “enlightened ones” began the Propaganda Movement that fought for equality and freedom of Filipinos. A notable figure in this movement is Jose Rizal who ignited the Filipinos to initiate the Philippine Revolution.

American Rule

After the Spanish Colony yielded (end of the Spanish-American War), rather than giving the Philippines its independence, it ended up in a Philippine-American War due to the Treaty of Paris that states that the Philippines is now claimed by America. The motivations of the Americans were to have commercial opportunities in Asia. They thought of Filipinos as having inadequate skills to self-govern and feared that another power (Germany or Japan) might take control of the Philippines. So, the Americans launched a pacification, benevolent assimilation, that aims to gain the trust of the Filipinos by giving them self-governance, social reforms, and education rights. In 1946, the U.S. granted the Philippines its independence.

Japanese Rule

During WWII, Japan attacked and conquered the Philippines from 1941-1945. Atrocities occurred such as the countless rape and torture of comfort women, mass murders involving priests, nuns, professionals, and even children, and lastly, the painful history of the Bataan Death March. February to March 1945, marks the end of the Japanese occupation by the efforts of both Filipinos and American troops in the Battle of Manila.

Geography & Environment

The Philippines consists of more than 7,000 islands (7,640 to be precise) that form an archipelago. The topography of the Philippines is mountainous and has narrow to large coastal lowlands, depending on each island. The Philippines has divided into three geographic areas mainly Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. The environment/climate of the Philippines is tropical marine which has a rainy and dry seasonal climate.

Culture


Filipino Values

Filipinos are known to uphold many significant values that revolve around and are rooted in relationships and societal obligations. At early ages, Filipinos are taught to be respectful toward elders; such as saying “po” and “opo” and the “pagmamano” which is raising the back of the hand of their elders to their foreheads. Filipinos are also known to be loving, incorporating a love for God, family,and one’s neighbor (the practice of hospitality to guests or strangers).

Filipino Cuisine

Filipino Cuisine is heavily influenced by Spanish, Indian, Chinese, and Native flavors ranging from sweet, salty, and flavorful meals that offer a variety of delicacies. Filipinos are known to be heavy eaters; They meal 6 times a day – breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks after those meals mentioned. Some popular dishes are Sinigang, Adobo, Lechon, and many more.

Filipino Tradition and Arts

Filipinos celebrate different festivals such as Ati-atihan, Sinulog, Masskara, and many more that focus on the richness of religion, culture, and beliefs. This is usually done with different local delicacies, music, floats, and dances. This also leads us to the richness of art, literature, and dances of the Philippines. Notable figures are Fernando Amorsolo (Artist - Painter) and Jose Rizal (Writer).
